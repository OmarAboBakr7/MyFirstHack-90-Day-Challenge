**How to explain cybersecurity simply?**
Protecting personal digital life, computers, networks, online data and other people or companies from digital attacks.
## Why This Matters?

A single password leak can cause massive financial losses to a company and everyone it provides a service or product to.

**Where do most cyberattacks originate from?**
Weak passwords or suspicious emails

**Is it difficult to learn the skills to protect against most cyberattacks?**
No, since most aren't sophisticated and rely on basic mistakes that anyone can learn how not to make
## What You'd Actually Do Every Day?

**There are two main career paths**
**The same foundation prepares for both paths**
### Path 1: SOC Analyst

**What is SOC short for?**
Security Operations Centre

**What do they do?**
- Detect alarms of possible threats to the network they're protecting
- Do so through a SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) dashboard

> It's basically detective work, but with computers.

### Path 2: GRC Analyst

**What is GRC short for?**
Governance, Risk & Compliance

**What do they do?**
- Their work is less technical and more analytical
- They check if their company follows GDPR properly
- They assess whether a new software vendor is a security risk
- They write a security policy that five thousand employees need to follow
- They make sure their company doesn't get fined millions for breaking data protection laws

# Hands-On Experience

## Task 1

### Find Out What's Already Out There

Here's a fun little wake-up call: some of your data is almost certainly already floating around the dark web, and you've never been told.

Head to haveibeenpwned.com and type in your primary email. The site cross-checks it against every major breach on record and tells you exactly which companies leaked your info.

Most people walk away with 5-15 breaches they never heard about. Some find 30+. One of our testers found her email in a breach from a flower delivery site she used once in 2014.

> What to capture
> 
> - Total number of breaches your email appears in
> - Top 3 breach company names and the year each one happened
> - Your honest gut reaction (surprised? angry? weirdly numb?)

This is the exact first step cybersecurity professionals run for their clients. You're already doing the work — welcome to the field.

[Open HaveIBeenPwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com/)

## Task 3
### See What the Jobs Actually Want

Let's skip the guessing and go straight to the source: the people hiring for the jobs you want.

Open **LinkedIn** in a new tab, head to the Jobs section, and search **"SOC Analyst"** in your area. Open 3 listings. Then search **"GRC Analyst"** and open 2 more.

Scroll to the "Requirements" section of each one. You're going to see the same words again and again — SIEM, Splunk, ISO 27001, incident response, risk assessment. Right now they sound like a foreign language. That's the whole point.

> What to capture
> 
> - The 5 most common requirements you see repeated across all 5 listings
> 1. SIEM Platforms (e.g., Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel)
> 2. Incident Response (IR)
> 3. Cybersecurity & Risk Frameworks (e.g., ISO 27001, NIST CSF)
> 4. Risk Assessments & Vendor Management
> 5. Network & Log Analysis (TCP/IP, Wireshark, Windows/Linux Logs)

Keep the list. Over the next 6 months, we're ticking every single one of those off. When you re-read it on Day 90, you'll recognize every word on that list — and most of them will be things you can actually do.
